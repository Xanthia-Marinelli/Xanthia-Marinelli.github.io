Leather Costral

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

A traditional leather costral or flask, hand-stitched and shaped via leather wet moulding to create a rounded body and narrow neck. The surface is decorated with a tooled tree of life design enclosed in a circular border of leaf motifs, giving it a rustic, artisanal look. It has a cork stopper secured by laced leather cords, with a braided strap for carrying over the shoulder. Its finished inside and out with bees wax for water-proofing and protection. Its ideal for medieval reenactments, fantasy costumes, or rustic outdoor use.

Skills Showcased:

  • Historic Textiles Knowledge
  • Properties and Performance of Fabric and Fibres
  • Leather Skills
  • Pattern Making
  • Hand Sewing (Saddle Stitch)
  • Wet Moulding
  • Leather Tooling
  • Natural Dying
  • Water-proofing

Textiles Case Studies

Alone Task Gear Case Study

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)

This “Alone” Case Study is about survival gear and textiles, focusing on how specific materials such as merino wool, paracord, and leather are used to support human survival in harsh environments. It explores their properties, performance, and sustainability, highlighting how these materials provide warmth, strength, durability, and functionality in situations like arctic climates or wilderness survival. It connects material science and textiles to real-world survival applications.

Skills Showcased:

  • Analyse & evaluate textiles for specific uses based on properties and performance.
  • Describe textiles in detail using correct terminology (functional vs aesthetic).
  • Recognise value of textiles to individuals and society (social, cultural, philosophical).
  • Apply technical knowledge (fibres, yarns, fabric construction, finishes)
  • Apply design principles to textiles in hypothetical scenarios.
  • Support ideas with evidence and clear examples.
  • Research & organise information effectively.

Investigating the Thermal Conductivity of Textiles

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

A scientific laboratory report investigating the thermal conductivity of textiles. It explores how thickness (neoprene) and humidity (wool) affect the rate at which heat is transferred. The experiment measures how quickly water cools when insulated with different textile samples. Results showed that thicker neoprene slowed heat loss, while wool with higher humidity (wet wool) conducted heat faster than dry wool.

Skills Showcased:

  • Formulates hypotheses and makes predictions.
  • Follows scientific method and conducts experiments.
  • Collects and records data accurately across trials.
  • Analyses results using tables, graphs, and averages.
  • Applies theory (heat transfer, textile properties) to findings.
  • Evaluates limitations and suggests improvements.
  • Uses correct terminology for textiles and science.
  • Communicates clearly with structured explanations and visuals.
  • Writes in academic format (aim, method, results, discussion, conclusion).
  • Researches and references credible academic sources.
