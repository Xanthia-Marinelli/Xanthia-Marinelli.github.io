Woodworking Projects

Project Title:

Phone or Storage Box

Brief Description:

A rectangular handcrafted wooden storage box, made from pine wood. It has a a clear acrylic top that slides on and off, engraved with a dog and geometric designs.

Skills Showcased:

  • Practical Woodworking Skills: measuring, marking out, sawing, sanding, planing, drilling, and assembling timber components.
  • Safe Tool and Machine use: correct operation of hand tools (tenon saw, hammer, plane, try square, vice) and power tools (disc sander, drill press, table saw, laser cutter) with appropriate PPE.
  • Technical Accuracy: ensuring precise dimensions, flush joins, square edges, correct pilot holes, and smooth finishes.
  • Problem-solving: making adjustments (e.g., removing lips, sanding to flush, filling gaps) to ensure proper fit and finish.
  • Digital Design & Technology skills: using Fusion 360 and Adobe Illustrator to design and decorate the lid, preparing files for the laser cutter.
  • Assembly and Finishing Techniques: gluing, nailing, using putty, and sanding through progressive grits to achieve a professional outcome.

Best Suited For:

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)

Project Title:

Froggy Toy

Brief Description:

A handcrafted small wooden figurine with a rectangular head, circular eyes, and flexible cord arms and legs with wooden block hands and feet. This toy works in part with the Rubber-Band Racer Project from Design & Technology.

Skills Showcased:

Project Title:

Chopping Board

Brief Description:

A handcrafted chopping board utilising Tasmanian Blackwood and White Oak with a laser engraved makers mark. The shape of the board is rectangular with four rounded edges that never intersect.

Skills Showcased:

  • Measuring and Marking out.
  • Cutting and Jointing: safely cutting timber and joining contrasting woods (lamination).
  • Squaring and Planning: achieving flat, even surfaces before rounding.
  • Surface preparation: sanding progressively for a smooth finish.
  • Finishing Techniques: applying oil/sealant to protect and highlight the timber grain.
  • Tool Use: safe use of saws, clamps, planers, sanders.

Project Title:

Bridging Time and Design: Interpreting Historical and Cultural Details into Contemporary Furniture

Brief Description:

A report that explores how historical and cultural influences from periods such as the Renaissance, Baroque, and Art Nouveau continue to shape contemporary furniture. It examines the shift from handcrafted, ornate pieces to mass-produced, trend-driven designs, and highlights how modern designers merge traditional craftsmanship with new technologies and consumer needs to create functional yet timeless furniture.

Skills Showcased:

  • Investigating historical and cultural design influences.
  • Comparing past and present design movements.
  • Linking historical styles to modern design practices.
  • Clearly expressing complex ideas in writing.
  • Using correct terminology, structure, and referencing.
  • Drawing connections between tradition, technology, and innovation.

Project Title:

Jewellers Hammer

Brief Description:

A jeweller’s hammer with a dual-metal head, featuring one brass striking face and one aluminium face for versatile use. Its blue, red, and clear layered acrylic handle, reinforced with steel components, is shaped and polished to be both durable and visually striking.

Skills Showcased:

  • Cutting and shaping brass, aluminium and steel using a cold saw and lathe.
  • Drilling precise holes for fitting the head using the lathe and drill mill machine.
  • Polishing metal surfaces for durability and function.
  • Bonding layered acrylic sheets with epoxy.
  • Shaping the handle to an ergonomic form the lathe.
  • Creating threads with hand taps.
  • Communicates ideas effectively with accurate terminology.
  • Applying finishing techniques for both function and aesthetics.
