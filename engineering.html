Engineering Projects

Automated Pot Plant

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

An automatic self-watering pot plant using Arduino and sensors and 3D printing. The system was created to help people who forget to water their plants or are away from home, by making sure the soil always has the right moisture level. The project aimed to combine technology, sustainability, and design to solve a real-world problem in a creative way.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research & problem solving: investigating plant care challenges and smart irrigation solutions.
  • Electronics & coding: wiring sensors, pump, and LEDs, and programming the Arduino.
  • 3D design & fabrication: modelling, 3D printing, waterproofing, and assembling the pot.
  • Testing & troubleshooting: calibrating sensors, fixing wiring, and adjusting code.
  • Creativity & innovation: designing a futuristic, functional planter with hidden electronics that looks aesthetically pleasing.
  • Evaluation: reflecting on what worked, what failed, and suggesting improvements.

Engineering Case Studies

Circuit Report

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

This case study focused on understanding how electrical circuits and sensors work. The first part was a case study on an electric kettle, explaining how a thermostat sensor detects steam pressure and shuts off the heating element once the water boils. The second part involved analysing a light-dependent resistor (LDR) circuit using Ohm’s Law to calculate current, voltage, resistance, and power under different lighting conditions. Finally, the project applied this knowledge to predict when an LED would light up in a circuit, depending on light intensity.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research: investigating how everyday devices like kettles use sensors.
  • Electronics knowledge: understanding resistors, sensors, and how current flows.
  • Mathematical skills: applying Ohm’s Law to calculate voltage, current, and power.
  • Problem solving: predicting how circuits behave under different conditions.
  • Critical thinking: explaining why current takes the path of least resistance..
  • Communication: presenting findings clearly with diagrams, explanations, and summaries.
