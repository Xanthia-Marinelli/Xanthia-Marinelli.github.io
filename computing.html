Project Title:

Farmbot Automatic Water Monitoring System Analysis

Brief Description:

This project researched the Farmbot Automatic Water Monitoring System, an Internet of Things (IoT) device used in Australian agriculture. Farmbot uses solar-powered sensors placed in water tanks, dams, and bores to track water levels and transmit real-time data to farmers through the MyFarmbot app. The system helps farmers save time, reduce costs, and improve water management by allowing them to monitor tanks remotely and even control pumps from their phones. The study looked at how Farmbot works, its benefits (efficiency, sustainability, safety) and its challenges (reliance on Wi-Fi/satellite connectivity and data privacy concerns). It showed how digital technologies like IoT can transform agriculture, especially in remote areas, but also highlighted the limitations of infrastructure in rural Australia.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research skills: investigating how IoT and smart sensors work in farming.
  • Digital technologies knowledge: understanding cloud data, apps, and wireless systems.
  • Critical thinking: weighing up benefits (efficiency, sustainability) and risks (connectivity, privacy).
  • Communication: presenting findings in a clear, structured research report.
  • Problem solving: analysing how tech solutions could improve water monitoring challenges.
  • Reflection & evaluation: considering the future of digital agriculture and possible improvements.

Best Suited For:

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)
