Food Projects

Flavour Forcast Challenge

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)

My take on the Flavour Forcast Challenge which is a global food trends report published every year. It highlights emerging flavours, ingredients, and cooking techniques that are expected to shape the way people cook and eat in the future. My creation is a vegetarian Mediterranean breakfast dish. The meal included homemade flatbread, roasted and grilled vegetables, eggs, labneh, feta, and dukkah seasoning. It was created to meet time limits, dietary guidelines, and presentation standards while keeping costs realistic.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research into global food trends.
  • Food preparation: cooking with a range of fresh ingredients and techniques.
  • Recipe writing: creating clear, step-by-step instructions.
  • Costing & budgeting: calculating ingredient costs per serve.
  • Creativity: designing a colourful, balanced dish.
  • Time management: completing the dish within one hour.
  • Reflection & evaluation: evaluating taste, nutrition, and improvements for future attempts.

Diet Analysis Report

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)

This project analysed my personal diet over two days, comparing it to the Australian Dietary Guidelines and the Australian Eating Survey (AES). It looked at food groups, nutrient intake, and areas for improvement. I redesigned my meals to better balance food groups, lower sodium and sugar, and increase vegetables, nuts, and lean protein.

Skills Showcased:

  • Nutrition analysis: comparing food intake to dietary guidelines.
  • Data interpretation: using AES scores, charts, and nutrient breakdowns.
  • Nutrition & health knowledge: understanding macronutrients, micronutrients, and serving sizes.
  • Problem solving: redesigning meals to be healthier and more balanced.
  • Reflection & evaluation: evaluating habits and planning changes for better diet quality.
  • Communication: presenting findings clearly with diagrams, explanations, and summaries.

Vitamin C Titration Lab Report

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

This project investigated the vitamin C content in fruit and drinks by using titration with iodine and starch as an indicator. A standard curve was created using known concentrations of ascorbic acid, and then samples like orange juice, apples, capsicum, Berocca, and sports drinks were tested. Results showed large differences in vitamin C levels depending on the product.

Skills Showcased:

  • Scientific method: setting up controlled experiments and collecting data.
  • Chemistry skills: performing titrations accurately and recording results.
  • Data analysis: constructing standard curves and interpreting results.
  • Critical thinking: comparing experimental findings with packaging labels and databases.
  • Critical thinking: explaining why current takes the path of least resistance..
  • Communication: writing a clear lab report with tables, graphs, and discussion.
