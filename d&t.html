Design & Technology Projects

Rubber Band Racer

Stage 4 (Year 7 or 8)

A STEM-based design and engineering activity involving constructing a model car powered by the tension of rubber bands. The focus is on applying scientific principles of energy transfer, motion, and friction while developing practical skills.

Skills Showcased:

  • Design & planning skills using Fusion360.
  • Cutting & shaping using the laser cutter.
  • Assembly Skills.
  • Mechanics & Motion Understanding.
  • Testing & Evaluation.
  • Problem-Solving & Iteration.
  • Creativity & Aesthetics.

Creativity Card Game - Drawvolution

Any stage for playing / Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10) for creating their own card game

Drawvolution is a creative, educational, drawing game inspired by Telestrations and Pictionary. The game is designed to spark creative thinking and generate design ideas for students through the methods of SCAMPER, constraint based creativity, random input, rapid ideation and collaboration. The game works with a minimum of 4 players but can be played with up to 6 players.

Skills Showcased:

  • Creativity: coming up with fun ideas, prompts, and challenges.
  • Problem solving: fixing rules or designs when they don’t work properly.
  • Critical thinking: making sure the game is fair, balanced, and challenging.
  • Design skills: laying out cards so they look good and are easy to use.
  • Communication: writing clear instructions and explaining the game to others.
  • Collaboration: testing the game with others and using feedback to improve it.

Foam Model

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

The project is a 1:5 scale foam core model of a cupboard, created to test proportions, structure, and design features before constructing the real version. The model highlights an innovative feature: the cupboard has no back legs, allowing it to sit flush against a wall. This project demonstrates how designers use scaled prototypes to visualise and refine ideas before committing to full-scale builds, ensuring accuracy and efficiency in production.

Skills Showcased:

  • Scaling skills: turning a real idea into a scale model.
  • Measuring & building: cutting and joining foam core accurately.
  • Presentation: showing the model with photos and explanations.
  • Scaled hand technical drawing.

Narsil Sword Lamp

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

The design and construction of a wall lamp made in the style of the Shards of Narsil sword from The Lord of the Rings. It combines plywood, acrylic, and LED strips to create a sculptural light piece that works as both a piece of art and a functional light, appealing to LOTR fans who value unique memorabilia that fits into modern home décor. Prototyping, experimentation, and adjustments (like testing hanging methods and acrylic scaling) were central to the design process.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research: investigating lighting trends, LOTR fandom, and user needs.
  • Design skills: creating sketches, Fusion 360 models, and concept variations.
  • Experimentation: testing acrylic scaling, light diffusion, and hanging methods.
  • Construction skills: laser cutting plywood and acrylic, assembling components, and wiring LEDs.
  • Problem solving: adapting the design when materials broke or lighting didn’t diffuse properly.
  • Evaluation: reflecting on what worked, what didn’t, and proposing future improvements.
  • Creativity: merging fantasy storytelling with functional product design.

Design & Technology Case Studies

Design a Desk Case Study

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10) or Stage 6 (Year 11 or 12) if additionally making the desk

This case study focused on designing a modern, adaptable workstation for student accommodation at Foster College, which has 100 identical rooms. The case study goes through a full design process: initial research, market analysis, mood boards, concept sketches, prototyping, ergonomic checks, user testing, material analysis, costing, and evaluation. Student and external feedback guided iterative improvements, such as adjusting storage solutions, refining ergonomics, and enhancing sustainability.

Skills Showcased:

  • Research: interviewing students and analysing current desk designs.
  • Design thinking: turning feedback into a practical and creative solution.
  • Technical skills: sketching, rendering, and checking ergonomics.
  • Problem solving: adapting the desk for space, cost, and user needs.
  • Sustainability: choosing materials that are durable and eco-friendly.
  • Communication: presenting ideas clearly through drawings and reports.
  • Project management: planning materials, costs, and manufacturing steps.

Tomtoc Laptop Briefcase Decomposition

Stage 5 (Year 9 or 10)

A complex break down of the Tomtoc Defender-A40 Laptop Bag, designed for 14-inch laptops like the MacBook Pro to study how each feature contributes to its function, appearance, and user experience. The case study explored the bag’s purpose (protection and portability), design features (shock-absorbing padding, slim profile, compartments, adjustable strap), and materials (recycled polyester, nylon, PU leather, foam, plastic hardware). It also evaluated sustainability claims, looked at ergonomics and user comfort, and compared it to a premium leather briefcase.

Skills Showcased:

  • Product analysis: breaking down the bag into materials, features, and components.
  • Critical thinking: identifying strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for redesign.
  • Research skills: using statistics, sustainability reports, and product standards.
  • Design knowledge: applying the nine product design factors (function, user, innovation, aesthetics, sustainability, economics, legal, materials, technology).
  • Communication: presenting findings in a clear, professional report with diagrams and comparisons.
  • Evaluation: comparing products on the market and suggesting improvements (e.g., durability, sustainability, premium appeal).
  • Uses correct terminology for textiles and science.
  • Communicates clearly with structured explanations and visuals.
  • Writes in academic format (aim, method, results, discussion, conclusion).
  • Researches and references credible academic sources.
