Unit of Work - Stage 6 Textiles and Design

"Innovate to Advocate" is a unit where students research a social or environmental issue and design, construct, and present a textile item (such as a garment or accessory) that communicates their chosen message. Through project-based learning, they build skills in design, communication, and manufacturing while using textiles as a medium for advocacy, culminating in a Mini Major Project and process journal.

Resources - Innovate to Advocate Stage 6 Textiles and Design

These activities are part of the Year 11 “Innovate to Advocate – Textiles with a Message” unit, designed for a class with diverse textiles experience. The program uses differentiated, problem- and project-based learning to promote creativity, critical analysis, and reflection through real-world applications.

Resources - Wearable Technology Stage 6 Textiles and Design

These activities are part of the Year 11 “Wearable Technology” unit which explores how wearable design can address real-world needs through innovation and design thinking. Across structured activities aligned with the Stage 6 Design and Technology syllabus, students research, analyse, and develop original concepts while building skills in collaboration, problem-solving, and visual communication.
